It is the hypothetical source of/evidence for its existence is provided by: Sanskrit kama-muta "moved by love" and probably mivati "pushes, moves"; Greek ameusasthai "to surpass," amyno "push away"; Latin movere "move, set in motion"; Lithuanian mauti "push on."

Step to Unknown

Life rarely happens as predicted, given that we will never be able to anticipate everything. At no point can we have full control of external events. Staying in the realm of comfort and self-confirming practice makes us fragile and unable to thrive in the ever-changing universe.

Together, we can create an environment where instead of stabilizing the state of events, we ground ourselves in an unstable flow of things and embrace the unknown, expanding the horizons by doing things we struggle with.

Concept & Proposal

This is not a promise of a solving-all-magical-protocol; we are not interested in that. It's a humble call out to those who want to join a journey over the diverse landscape of life. A wish to open up, receive feedback, and build a movement collective based on shared curiosity to:

  • Examine life philosophy of motion and struggle through practice and questioning (why?, how?, what?)
  • Nourish organism beyond an individual a sum of
  • Search how such experience can improve our lives and relation to the surrounding world
  • Cultivate non-verbal connections and dialogue
  • Challenge ourselves and learn
  • Let go, play, fight and dance together
  • Explore different areas through body, mind, heart, and spirit: challenge, play, focus, coordination, balance, rhythm, improvisation, tension, relaxation, discipline, touch, strength, flow, speed, stillness, collaboration, competition, trust, flexibility, explosiveness, endurance, mobility, etc. You name it.

Practice

Let surprise be our northern star. Overall structure of a session may look like this:

  • Various movements consisting of some of these areas (60-75min):
    • Dynamic mobility
    • Coordination
    • Movement flows
    • Partnering & group practice
    • Play & improvise
    • Challenge
  • Strength & endurance (30-45min)
    • Skill over mass
    • Practice over workout
    • Use and process over numbers
  • Re-generation (remaining time)
  • Closing round (5min)
